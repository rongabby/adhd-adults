## Overview
An anti-toxic-positivity approach to gratitude and celebration, designed specifically for ADHD brains who struggle with traditional gratitude practices.

**This is NOT**:
- ‚ùå Forced positivity
- ‚ùå Ignoring real struggles
- ‚ùå "Just be grateful" toxicity

**This IS**:
- ‚úÖ Honest acknowledgment of both good and hard

## Why This Is Different

### Traditional Gratitude Practice Problems (for ADHD)
1. **Feels fake** when you're genuinely struggling
2. **Executive function drain** - "What am I grateful for?" is HARD
3. **Comparison trap** - "Others have it worse" = guilt
4. **Consistency pressure** - Miss a day = failure feeling
5. **Toxic positivity** - Forces you to find silver linings

### This Approach
1. **Both/and** - Can be hard AND have good moments
2. **Specific prompts** - No blank page paralysis
3. **Win-focused** - Doing things IS gratitude
4. **Flexible** - Skip when you need to
5. **Real** - Acknowledges the full picture

---

## How to Use This Journal

### Daily Practice (2-5 minutes)
- Complete what resonates, skip what doesn't
- Write as much or little as feels right
- Missing days is expected and okay
- Some days will be richer than others

### Weekly Practice (10 minutes)
- Look back at the week's entries
- Notice patterns (don't judge them)
- Celebrate that you showed up
- Adjust approach if needed

### Monthly Practice (20 minutes)
- Review the month
- Collect favorite moments
- See growth you can't see daily
- Document for future hard times

## FAQ

### "What if I can't find anything to be grateful for?"

That's okay and valid. On those days:
- Check what you survived (that's enough)
- Skip the gratitude prompt entirely
- Come back when you're ready
- Remember: Gratitude is not required for worth

### "I feel guilty when I miss days."

Permission to let that go:
- This journal is optional, not required
- Missing days doesn't negate your progress
- You can start fresh anytime
- Guilt serves no purpose here

### "I compare my wins to others and feel bad."

Your wins are valid at any scale:
- Someone else's marathon doesn't make your walk less valuable
- Different brains, different challenges, different wins
- The only comparison that matters: you vs past you
- Their journey isn't yours

### "Some days all I write is 'I got out of bed.'"

THAT COUNTS. Full stop.
- On hard days, that IS the win
- No win is too small
- Showing up is everything
- We celebrate that here
