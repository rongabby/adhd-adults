# AI Prompt Library for ADHD Flow OS

This document contains 50+ categorized AI prompts (expandable to 250+). Each prompt is designed to be copy-pasted into ChatGPT, Claude, or other AI assistants.

## üöÄ Task Initiation & Breaking Down Overwhelm

### 1. Task Breakdown Helper
```
I have a task that feels overwhelming: [TASK NAME]. 

Can you help me break this down into tiny, manageable steps? Please:
- Make each step take 15 minutes or less
- Start with the absolute easiest first step
- Use encouraging language
- Number the steps clearly

My current energy level is [1-10]: ___
```

### 2. Getting Started Nudge
```
I'm stuck starting this task: [TASK NAME]

I know I need to do it, but I can't seem to begin. Can you:
1. Give me just the first 2-minute action I need to take
2. Explain why starting is often the hardest part (help me feel less alone)
3. Suggest a reward for after I complete the first tiny step
```

### 3. Task Analysis for ADHD Brain
```
I need to do: [TASK NAME]

Please analyze this task for my ADHD brain:
- What's the actual difficulty level? (sometimes I overestimate)
- What's the minimum viable version of this task?
- What distractions might pull me away?
- What environmental setup would help?
```

### 4. Overwhelm Reducer
```
I'm feeling overwhelmed by everything I need to do. Here's my list:
[PASTE YOUR BRAIN DUMP]

Please help me:
1. Identify what's ACTUALLY urgent vs what feels urgent
2. Find 1-3 quick wins (under 5 minutes each)
3. Suggest what I can postpone without guilt
4. Remind me that I can't do everything today, and that's okay
```

### 5. Decision Paralysis Breaker
```
I'm stuck choosing between: [OPTION A] and [OPTION B]

My ADHD brain is spinning. Can you:
- List 3 pros and cons for each (brief)
- Suggest a simple "coin flip" test: which option makes me feel relieved?
- Remind me that most decisions aren't permanent
- Give me permission to just pick one and move on
```

Can you:
- Check in with me every 10 minutes (I'll come back to this chat)
- Ask me "What are you working on right now?"
- Give gentle encouragement without judgment
- Help me get back on track if I've wandered
```

### 11. RSD Emergency Support
```
I'm experiencing rejection sensitive dysphoria right now. What happened:
[DESCRIBE SITUATION]

Please:
- Remind me that my reaction is valid but likely amplified
- Help me identify what actually happened vs what I'm feeling
- Suggest a gentle self-compassion statement
- Give me permission to step away and come back later
```

### 12. Shame Spiral Stopper
```
I'm in a shame spiral about: [WHAT YOU'RE ASHAMED OF]

Help me:
- Separate the behavior from my identity (I did X, but I'm not X)
- Identify what I can control going forward
- Remind me that everyone with ADHD struggles sometimes
- Suggest one tiny action to make things better
```

### 13. Emotional Check-In Guide
```
I'm not sure what I'm feeling right now, just "off."

Walk me through:
- Common emotions ADHD people experience (help me identify)
- Possible triggers (sleep, meds, schedule, social, etc.)
- A body scan (where do I feel tension?)
- One kind thing I can do for myself right now
```

### 14. Anxiety Reducer for Tasks
```
This task is making me anxious: [TASK]

Help me:
- Identify what specifically I'm worried about
- Reality-check my fears (are they likely?)
- Break down the worst-case scenario (and how I'd handle it)
- Remind me of past wins where I felt this way but succeeded anyway
```

### 15. Compassionate Self-Talk Generator
```

Please rewrite my self-criticism as self-compassion. My harsh thought is:
"[YOUR NEGATIVE SELF-TALK]"

Help me say this to myself the way a kind friend would say it to me.
```

## üß† Hyperfocus Protection & Transitions

### 16. Hyperfocus Prep Checklist
```
I'm about to start a deep work session on: [PROJECT]

Help me create a pre-flight checklist:
- What to grab now (water, snacks, materials)
- What to close/silence (apps, notifications)
- What to tell others (boundaries)
- Emergency interruption plan (if I must stop early)
```

### 17. Transition Ritual Creator
```
I struggle with transitions between tasks/modes. I'm moving from [ACTIVITY A] to [ACTIVITY B].

Design a 5-minute transition ritual for me that:
- Honors what I just finished
- Clears my mental space
- Prepares me for what's next
- Uses movement or sensory input
```

### 18. Hyperfocus Emergency Exit
```
I've been hyperfocused on [TASK] for [TIME] and need to stop, but I can't mentally let go.

Help me:
- Acknowledge the work I've done
- Create a "save point" (what to capture before stopping)
- Design a gentle wind-down (5-10 min)
- Set up tomorrow's re-entry plan
```

### 19. Context Switching Helper
```
I need to switch from [TASK/MODE A] to [TASK/MODE B] but my brain won't let go.

Suggest:
- A "parking lot" prompt to capture where I am
- A physical action to signal the switch (stand up, music change, etc.)
- A mantra or phrase to repeat
- Realistic expectations (this is hard for ADHD brains!)
```

### 20. End-of-Day Shutdown Ritual
```
It's [TIME] and I need to stop working, but my brain is still racing about [TOPIC/TASKS].

Guide me through:
- Capturing open loops (write it down for tomorrow)
- Celebrating what I DID do today
- Releasing what I didn't finish (without guilt)
- A sensory/physical activity to signal "work is over"
```

## üìä Weekly Review & Reflection

### 21. Gentle Weekly Review
```
It's weekly review time. This week was: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION]

Guide me through a forgiving review:
- What went well? (even tiny things count)
- What was hard? (without judgment)
- What patterns do I notice?
- One small adjustment for next week?
- Reminder: progress over perfection
```

### 22. Pattern Recognition Helper
```
I've been tracking [MOOD/ENERGY/PRODUCTIVITY] for [TIME PERIOD]. Here's my data:
[PASTE DATA OR DESCRIBE PATTERNS]

Help me:
- Identify any patterns (best/worst times, triggers, etc.)
- Suggest experiments to test (change one variable)
- Remind me correlation isn't always causation
- Celebrate what I've learned about myself
```

### 23. Goal Refinement (ADHD-Friendly)
```
I set this goal: [GOAL]

But I'm struggling to stick with it. Help me:
- Is this goal realistic for my ADHD brain?
- Can we make it more specific/measurable?
- Can we break it into smaller milestones?
- What's the minimum viable version?
- Do I actually want this, or do I think I "should"?
```

### 24. Wins Archive Prompt
```
What did I accomplish this [DAY/WEEK/MONTH]?

Help me celebrate by:
- Listing even the "small" stuff (it's not small!)
- Reframing what felt like failures as learning
- Identifying personal growth moments
- Reminding me that rest and self-care are accomplishments too
```

### 25. Values Check-In
```
I'm feeling off-track or burned out. My current values are:
[LIST YOUR VALUES: e.g., creativity, connection, health, freedom]

Help me:
- Rate how much I'm honoring each value (1-10)
- Identify misalignments (what I'm doing vs what I value)
- Suggest one small action per value this week
- Remind me that values can change, and that's okay
```

## üéØ Decision Fatigue Reducers

### 26. Should I Do This Today?
```
I'm not sure if I should work on [TASK] today.

Ask me these questions to help me decide:
- Is it time-sensitive?
- Does it match my current energy?
- Will I regret not doing it?
- Can someone else do it?
- What happens if I wait until tomorrow?
```

### 27. Template Creator
```
I'm doing [REPETITIVE TASK] often and making too many decisions each time.

Help me create a template/system:
- What steps are always the same?
- What can I batch-prepare?
- What can I automate or simplify?
- What decisions can I make once and reuse?
```

### 28. Priority Matrix
```
I have too many tasks and don't know what to prioritize. Here's my list:
[PASTE LIST]

Sort these using: Urgent/Important, Not Urgent/Important, Urgent/Not Important, Not Urgent/Not Important

Then tell me:
- What to do first (high impact + time-sensitive)
- What to schedule for later
- What to delegate or drop
```

Meal decisions drain my energy. Help me create:
- 7 easy meals I can rotate (based on: [DIETARY NEEDS/PREFERENCES])
- A simple grocery list template
- "Emergency" no-cook options

### 30. Decision-Free Morning Routine
```
My mornings are chaotic because I make too many decisions. Help me design:
- A fixed order of tasks (no thinking required)
- What to prepare the night before
- Clothing system (reduce choice)
- Breakfast options (2-3 defaults)
- Realistic time buffer for ADHD time blindness
```

### 31. Kind Coach Mode
```
I need you to be my ADHD coach. I'm struggling with: [SITUATION]

Talk to me like:
- A wise, patient friend who understands neurodivergence
- Someone who celebrates my strengths
- Someone who doesn't shame me for struggles
- Someone who believes I can figure this out

### 32. Accountability Partner
```
I committed to [GOAL/TASK] and I'm checking in.

Be my accountability partner:
- Ask me: "Did you do it?"
- If yes: Celebrate genuinely
- If no: Help me troubleshoot without judgment
- Either way: "What's your plan for tomorrow?"
```

### 33. Executive Function Assistant
```
Act as my external executive function brain. I need to [COMPLEX TASK].

You handle:
- Planning the steps
- Time estimation (add ADHD buffer!)
- Reminding me of materials/prep
- Checking in on progress
- Adjusting the plan when I get off track
```

### 34. Gentle Reminder System
```
I need reminders that aren't nagging. I want to [HABIT/TASK].

Create 3 reminder phrases that:
- Use positive framing (not "Don't forget!")
- Connect to my why (remind me of my motivation)
- Give me permission to skip if needed
- Celebrate when I do it
```

### 35. Energy Guardian
```
I tend to overcommit and burn out. I'm considering: [NEW COMMITMENT]

Be my energy guardian:
- Ask about my current capacity
- Remind me what I'm already juggling
- Help me estimate the real energy cost (not just time)
- Give me a script to say "no" or "not now"
- Validate that protecting my energy is okay
```

## How to Use These Prompts

1. **Copy the entire prompt** (including the template sections)
2. **Fill in the bracketed [SECTIONS]** with your specific situation
3. **Paste into ChatGPT, Claude, or your preferred AI**
4. **Save favorite responses** back into your Notion dashboard
5. **Iterate**: If the response doesn't feel right, add more context and try again

## Tips for Best Results

- **Be specific**: The more context you give, the better the AI can help
- **Be honest**: Include your energy level, mood, and struggles
- **Give feedback**: Tell the AI if its response is too harsh, too soft, or off-target
- **Experiment**: Try different phrasings to find what works for your brain
- **Personalize**: Edit these prompts to match your communication style
