# AI Prompts - Emotional Regulation & RSD Support

## Overview
These prompts help manage emotional dysregulation, rejection sensitive dysphoria (RSD), and the intense emotions that often come with ADHD.

---

## Prompt 1: RSD Emergency Support

### When to Use
- Experiencing rejection sensitivity right now
- Criticism hitting harder than it should
- Feeling intense shame or embarrassment
- Need immediate emotional support

### The Prompt
```
I'm experiencing rejection sensitive dysphoria right now. What happened:
[DESCRIBE SITUATION - what was said/done, by whom, context]

Please:
- Remind me that my reaction is valid but likely amplified
- Help me identify what actually happened vs what I'm feeling
- Suggest a gentle self-compassion statement
- Give me permission to step away and come back later
- Help me reality-check if this is truly rejection

My RSD is telling me: [your catastrophic thoughts]
Objectively, what happened: [try to state just facts]
```

### Example Usage
```
I'm experiencing rejection sensitive dysphoria right now. What happened:
My boss sent a short email saying "We need to talk tomorrow" with no other context.

Please:
- Remind me that my reaction is valid but likely amplified
- Help me identify what actually happened vs what I'm feeling
- Suggest a gentle self-compassion statement
- Give me permission to step away and come back later
- Help me reality-check if this is truly rejection

My RSD is telling me: I'm going to get fired, I messed up everything, they hate me
Objectively, what happened: I received a brief email requesting a meeting tomorrow
```

### Copy-Ready Version (Minimal)
```
RSD triggered by: [SITUATION]
My brain says: [CATASTROPHIC THOUGHTS]
What actually happened: [FACTS ONLY]
Help me: reality check, self-compassion, permission to step away
```

## Prompt 2: Shame Spiral Stopper

### When to Use
- Stuck in shame about a mistake or failure
- Can't stop ruminating
- Feeling like a bad person
- Need to separate behavior from identity

### The Prompt
```
I'm in a shame spiral about: [WHAT YOU'RE ASHAMED OF]

The story I'm telling myself: [Your harsh self-narrative]

Help me:
- Separate the behavior from my identity (I did X, but I'm not X)
- Identify what I can control going forward
- Remind me that everyone with ADHD struggles sometimes
- Suggest one tiny action to make things better
- Give me a self-compassion mantra

Context that might be important: [Why this happened, any ADHD factors]
```

### Example Usage
```
I'm in a shame spiral about: Forgetting my friend's birthday completely

The story I'm telling myself: I'm a terrible friend, I don't care about people, I always mess up relationships

Help me:
- Separate the behavior from my identity (I did X, but I'm not X)
- Identify what I can control going forward
- Remind me that everyone with ADHD struggles sometimes
- Suggest one tiny action to make things better
- Give me a self-compassion mantra

Context that might be important: ADHD time blindness, had a stressful week, this isn't intentional
```

### Copy-Ready Version (Minimal)
```
Shame about: [SITUATION]
Telling myself: [HARSH STORY]
Help: separate behavior from identity, what I can control, self-compassion, next small action
```

## Prompt 3: Emotional Check-In Guide

### When to Use
- Feeling "off" but can't identify the emotion
- Emotionally overwhelmed
- Need help processing feelings
- Want to understand what you're experiencing

### The Prompt
```
I'm not sure what I'm feeling right now, just "off" or overwhelmed.

Help me identify by asking questions:
- What emotions might I be experiencing? (provide a list to consider)
- Where do I feel this in my body?
- What might have triggered this?
- Is this feeling proportional to the situation?
- What does this feeling need from me right now?

Recent context: [Anything that's been happening]
Physical sensations: [What you notice in your body]
```

### Example Usage
```
I'm not sure what I'm feeling right now, just "off" or overwhelmed.

Help me identify by asking questions:
- What emotions might I be experiencing? (provide a list to consider)
- Where do I feel this in my body?
- What might have triggered this?
- Is this feeling proportional to the situation?
- What does this feeling need from me right now?

Recent context: Big project deadline tomorrow, didn't sleep well, skipped lunch
Physical sensations: Tight chest, racing heart, restless legs
```

### Copy-Ready Version (Minimal)
```
Feeling "off" but don't know why
Recent context: [WHAT'S BEEN HAPPENING]
Body sensations: [WHAT YOU FEEL PHYSICALLY]
Help me identify: emotions, triggers, needs
```

## Prompt 4: Anxiety Reducer for Tasks

### When to Use
- Task is making you anxious
- Catastrophizing about outcomes
- Fear is blocking action
- Need perspective

### The Prompt
```
This task is making me anxious: [TASK]

What I'm worried about:
- [Fear 1]
- [Fear 2]
- [Fear 3]

Help me:
- Identify what specifically I'm worried about (get granular)
- Reality-check my fears (are they likely?)
- Break down the worst-case scenario (and how I'd handle it)
- Remind me of past wins where I felt this way but succeeded anyway
- Find the smallest step that feels safe

My anxiety level: [1-10]
```

### Example Usage
```
This task is making me anxious: Presenting at the team meeting tomorrow

What I'm worried about:
- I'll freeze and forget what to say
- People will think I'm incompetent
- I'll say something stupid

Help me:
- Identify what specifically I'm worried about (get granular)
- Reality-check my fears (are they likely?)
- Break down the worst-case scenario (and how I'd handle it)
- Remind me of past wins where I felt this way but succeeded anyway
- Find the smallest step that feels safe

My anxiety level: 8/10
```

### Copy-Ready Version (Minimal)
```
Anxious about: [TASK]
Fears: [LIST]
Anxiety level: [X/10]
Help: reality check, worst-case analysis, past wins, safest first step
```

## Prompt 5: Compassionate Self-Talk Generator

### When to Use
- Being harsh with yourself
- Negative self-talk is loud
- Need to reframe criticism as compassion
- Want to speak to yourself like a friend would

### The Prompt
```
I'm being really hard on myself about: [SITUATION]

My harsh inner critic says:
"[YOUR NEGATIVE SELF-TALK - copy it exactly as you hear it]"

Please:
1. Acknowledge what's hard about this (validate the struggle)
2. Rewrite this as self-compassion (the way a kind friend would say it)
3. Add context about ADHD if relevant
4. Give me permission to be human
5. Suggest a kind action I can take for myself right now

I need the compassionate version to be: [gentle/firm but kind/playfully loving/etc.]
```

### Example Usage
```
I'm being really hard on myself about: Not finishing my project on time

My harsh inner critic says:
"You're so lazy. Everyone else can get things done. What's wrong with you? You always fail."

Please:
1. Acknowledge what's hard about this (validate the struggle)
2. Rewrite this as self-compassion (the way a kind friend would say it)
3. Add context about ADHD if relevant
4. Give me permission to be human
5. Suggest a kind action I can take for myself right now

I need the compassionate version to be: gentle but honest
```

### Copy-Ready Version (Minimal)
```
Being harsh about: [SITUATION]
Inner critic says: "[EXACT HARSH WORDS]"
Rewrite as: compassionate self-talk, validate struggle, ADHD context, permission to be human
```

## Prompt 6: Meltdown Prevention Plan

### When to Use
- Feeling a meltdown building
- Overwhelmed and about to shut down
- Need emergency de-escalation
- Want to prevent emotional explosion

### The Prompt
```
I feel a meltdown building. Current state:

Physical: [racing heart, tense muscles, hot face, etc.]
Emotional: [anger, sadness, overwhelm, etc.]
Mental: [can't think, racing thoughts, blank, etc.]
Situation: [what's happening around me]

I need:
- Immediate grounding techniques (give me 3 options)
- Permission to remove myself if needed
- A script for what to say to others
- Reminder that this will pass
- What to do AFTER I regulate

Time I have before meltdown: [estimate in minutes]
```

### Example Usage
```
I feel a meltdown building. Current state:

Physical: Racing heart, jaw clenched, want to cry
Emotional: Overwhelming frustration and sadness
Mental: Can't think clearly, everything feels too much
Situation: At work, just got critical feedback, have more meetings today

I need:
- Immediate grounding techniques (give me 3 options)
- Permission to remove myself if needed
- A script for what to say to others
- Reminder that this will pass
- What to do AFTER I regulate

Time I have before meltdown: Maybe 5-10 minutes
```

### Copy-Ready Version (Minimal)
```
Meltdown building
Physical/Emotional/Mental: [CURRENT STATE]
Time left: [ESTIMATE]
Need: grounding NOW, permission to leave, scripts, post-regulation plan
```

## Prompt 7: Emotional Intensity Dialer

### When to Use
- Emotions feel too big
- Need to turn down the volume
- Can't match emotional response to situation
- Want to find equilibrium

### The Prompt
```
I'm feeling [EMOTION] at a level [1-10] right now.

The situation: [What happened]
My emotional reaction: [How intense it feels]
What a "proportional" response might be: [Your guess, if you have one]

Help me:
- Understand why this hit so hard (ADHD emotional intensity context)
- Validate that my feelings are real (even if amplified)
- Suggest ways to "turn down the volume" without dismissing the emotion
- Identify what this emotion might be trying to tell me
- Find a way to honor the feeling while functioning

I need to: [what you have to do next - work, talk to someone, make decision, etc.]
```

### Example Usage
```
I'm feeling ANGER at a level 9/10 right now.

The situation: Someone made a small joke about my lateness
My emotional reaction: Feels like a huge betrayal, want to yell, considering ending friendship
What a "proportional" response might be: Maybe a 3 or 4? Mild annoyance?

Help me:
- Understand why this hit so hard (ADHD emotional intensity context)
- Validate that my feelings are real (even if amplified)
- Suggest ways to "turn down the volume" without dismissing the emotion
- Identify what this emotion might be trying to tell me
- Find a way to honor the feeling while functioning

I need to: Stay calm and respond appropriately, not damage this friendship
```

### Copy-Ready Version (Minimal)
```
Emotion: [NAME] at [X/10]
Situation: [WHAT HAPPENED]
"Should" be: [X/10]
Help: why so intense, validate, turn down volume, what it's telling me, how to function
```

## Prompt 8: Post-Conflict Processing

### When to Use
- After an argument or difficult conversation
- Replaying the interaction obsessively
- Not sure if you reacted appropriately
- Need to process and move forward

### The Prompt
```
I just had a difficult interaction and I'm replaying it on loop.

What happened: [Describe the conflict or conversation]
What I said/did: [Your actions]
What they said/did: [Their actions]
How I feel now: [Current emotions]

Help me:
- Process what actually happened (separate facts from feelings)
- Identify if I need to repair anything
- Reality-check if I "messed up" as badly as I think
- Decide if I should reach out or let it be
- Give me permission to move on (or a plan if I need to address it)
- Remind me that conflict is part of relationships

My RSD is saying: [Your catastrophic interpretation]
```

### Example Usage
```
I just had a difficult interaction and I'm replaying it on loop.

What happened: Disagreed with coworker about project approach in meeting
What I said/did: Stated my opinion firmly, maybe interrupted them
What they said/did: Looked annoyed, said "fine whatever," left quickly
How I feel now: Convinced I ruined the relationship, they hate me, I was too aggressive

Help me:
- Process what actually happened (separate facts from feelings)
- Identify if I need to repair anything
- Reality-check if I "messed up" as badly as I think
- Decide if I should reach out or let it be
- Give me permission to move on (or a plan if I need to address it)
- Remind me that conflict is part of relationships

My RSD is saying: They hate me, I'm too much, I can't disagree without destroying relationships
```

### Copy-Ready Version (Minimal)
```
Conflict with: [PERSON]
What happened: [FACTS]
RSD says: [CATASTROPHIC THOUGHTS]
Help: separate facts/feelings, need to repair?, reality check, move on or address?
```

## Prompt 9: Overstimulation Recovery

### When to Use
- Sensory or emotional overload
- Too much input, can't process
- Need to decompress
- Shutdown mode activated

### The Prompt
```
I'm in sensory/emotional overload right now.

What led to this:
- [List triggers: noise, people, demands, emotions, etc.]

Current state:
- Can I think clearly? [Yes/No/Barely]
- Can I communicate verbally? [Yes/No/Barely]
- Can I move? [Yes/No/Barely]
- Am I safe where I am? [Yes/No]

I need:
- Immediate relief strategies (that work for current state)
- Permission to shut down if needed
- How long this usually lasts (reassurance it's temporary)
- What to do if I can't remove myself from situation
- Recovery plan for after

Time/space I have: [Describe your current situation]
```

### Example Usage
```
I'm in sensory/emotional overload right now.

What led to this:
- Loud crowded event for 3 hours
- Too many social interactions
- Bright lights and strong smells
- Had to mask the whole time

Current state:
- Can I think clearly? Barely
- Can I communicate verbally? No, too much
- Can I move? Yes but moving slowly
- Am I safe where I am? Yes, just got home

I need:
- Immediate relief strategies (that work for current state)
- Permission to shut down if needed
- How long this usually lasts (reassurance it's temporary)
- What to do if I can't remove myself from situation
- Recovery plan for after

Time/space I have: Home alone, can rest
```

### Copy-Ready Version (Minimal)
```
Overload from: [TRIGGERS]
Current capacity: think [Y/N], talk [Y/N], move [Y/N]
Safe? [Y/N], Can leave? [Y/N]
Need: immediate relief, permission to shut down, how long?, recovery plan
```

## Prompt 10: Emotional Reset Ritual

### When to Use
- Need to transition between emotional states
- Carrying emotion from one situation to another
- Want to "shake it off" but don't know how
- Need closure on a feeling

### The Prompt
```
I need to emotionally reset. I'm carrying [EMOTION] from [SITUATION] and I need to move into [NEXT SITUATION/STATE].

Current emotional state: [How you feel now]
Desired emotional state: [How you need to feel]
Time I have for reset: [Minutes available]

Create a reset ritual for me that includes:
- Physical movement or grounding
- Mental/cognitive shift
- Permission to let go
- Transition statement I can say
- Bonus: A symbolic action (if time allows)

My usual helpful tools: [music, movement, breathing, nature, etc.]
```

### Example Usage
```
I need to emotionally reset. I'm carrying frustration from work meeting and I need to move into family dinner.

Current emotional state: Angry, tense, irritated
Desired emotional state: Calm, present, warm
Time I have for reset: 15 minutes

Create a reset ritual for me that includes:
- Physical movement or grounding
- Mental/cognitive shift
- Permission to let go
- Transition statement I can say
- Bonus: A symbolic action (if time allows)

My usual helpful tools: Walking, music, deep breathing, changing clothes
```

### Copy-Ready Version (Minimal)
```
Need reset from: [CURRENT EMOTION/SITUATION]
Need to be: [DESIRED STATE]
Time: [MINUTES]
Create ritual: movement, mental shift, permission, transition statement, symbolic action
```

## Building Your Emotional Toolkit

### Create Your Emergency Kit
1. **Save these prompts** in easy-access location
2. **Practice when calm** so they're familiar in crisis
3. **Add personal variations** based on what works
4. **Share with trusted people** who support you
5. **Update regularly** as you learn what helps

### Track Patterns
- What triggers RSD most often?
- What emotions amplify most?
- What techniques work best for you?
- Who/what helps you regulate?
- What warnings signs precede meltdowns?

## When to Seek Additional Help

### These prompts are NOT a replacement for:
- Mental health crisis intervention
- Therapy for trauma or severe anxiety
- Medication management
- Emergency services

### Signs you need more support:
- Emotions regularly interfere with daily life
- RSD episodes are increasing
- Can't function due to emotional intensity
- Experiencing suicidal thoughts (call crisis line immediately)
- Self-harm urges
- Inability to maintain relationships
