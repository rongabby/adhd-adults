#### Day 1: Brain Dump Liberation

‚è±Ô∏è Time: 10 minutes
üí™ Energy: Low (perfect for starting)
üéÅ Reward: Favorite beverage or snack

üí™ Energy: Low (just checking in)

1. Set 6 alarms throughout your day (e.g., 8am, 11am, 2pm, 5pm, 8pm, 11pm)

3. Note what you were doing
4. At end of day, look for patterns

Step-by-Step:
1. List tasks you struggle to do consistently
2. Assign points based on difficulty (not importance)
3. Create reward tiers (10 pts, 25 pts, 50 pts, 100 pts)
4. List rewards you genuinely want
5. Set up in Notion or app of choice

‚ú® ADHD Tips:
- Rewards should feel good, not "should do" activities
- Start with easy wins (collect points fast)
- Reset weekly so you never feel too behind
- Change rewards when they stop being motivating

‚è±Ô∏è Time: 20 minutes
üí™ Energy: Medium-High

1. Review: What days/strategies worked best?
2. Identify: What can you commit to weekly?
3. Simplify: What can you let go of without guilt?
4. Customize: Adjust your Notion dashboard based on learnings

‚ú® ADHD Tips:
- You don't have to keep doing all 30 things
- Choose 3-5 practices to continue
- It's okay if you only did 15 of 30 days‚Äîthat's still a win!
- Schedule your next check-in (maybe monthly?)

## 2. Values & Identity Clarifier

### Overview
A deep-dive workbook to help ADHD adults reconnect with their authentic selves, separate from productivity culture and "should" obligations.

### Section 1: Who Am I Beyond My ADHD?

#### Exercise 1.1: Identity Statements
Complete these sentences:
- When I'm at my best, I am...
- People who really know me would describe me as...
- I feel most like myself when I'm...
- My ADHD gives me the gift of...
- Without external pressure, I naturally gravitate toward...

#### Exercise 1.2: Strengths Inventory (ADHD-Positive Frame)
Common ADHD strengths‚Äîwhich are yours?
- ‚òê Hyperfocus on interests
- ‚òê Creative problem-solving
- ‚òê High energy and enthusiasm
- ‚òê Thinking outside the box
- ‚òê Empathy and emotional depth
- ‚òê Resilience (you've adapted to so much!)
- ‚òê Spontaneity and fun
- ‚òê Justice-oriented thinking
- ‚òê Ability to see connections others miss

Rate each 1-10 and give an example of when you used this strength.

### Section 2: Uncovering Your Core Values

#### Exercise 2.1: Values Card Sort
[Provide list of 50+ values: e.g., adventure, security, creativity, connection, freedom, growth, etc.]

Instructions:
1. First pass: Mark any that resonate (no limit)
2. Second pass: Narrow to top 10
3. Third pass: Identify top 5
4. Final pass: Force-rank top 3

For each top 3, write:
- What this value means to you specifically
- When you honor this value, you feel...
- When you ignore this value, you feel...
- One way you can honor this value this week

#### Exercise 2.2: Values Conflict Resolution
Do any of your top values conflict? (e.g., adventure vs. security, freedom vs. connection)

For each conflict:
- Describe the tension
- Both values are valid‚Äîhow can they coexist?
- What season of life are you in? Which needs more emphasis now?

### Section 3: Who Am I Without Burnout?

#### Exercise 3.1: Burnout Inventory
Rate 1-10 (1=not at all, 10=extremely):
- I feel exhausted most of the time
- I've lost interest in things I used to love
- I feel cynical or resentful
- I struggle to accomplish basic tasks
- I feel disconnected from others
- I use numbing behaviors (excess social media, substance use, etc.)

If you scored 40+, you're likely experiencing burnout.

#### Exercise 3.2: Burnout Root Causes
What's driving your burnout? Check all that apply:
- ‚òê Overcommitment (saying yes to too much)
- ‚òê Perfectionism (nothing is ever good enough)
- ‚òê Lack of boundaries (others' needs always come first)
- ‚òê Identity tied to productivity (I am what I achieve)
- ‚òê Unmanaged ADHD challenges (constant overwhelm)
- ‚òê Unsupportive environment (work, relationships, etc.)
- ‚òê Ignoring my needs (rest, fun, connection, health)

For each checked, write one small boundary or change you could try.

#### Exercise 3.3: Permission Slips
Write yourself permission for the things you need to heal:

"I give myself permission to..."
- Rest without earning it
- Change my mind
- Disappoint others to protect my peace
- Take things off my plate
- Ask for help
- Be "unproductive" sometimes
- Prioritize joy
- [Your addition]

Sign and date this. Return to it when guilt creeps in.

### Section 4: Designing Your Aligned Life

#### Exercise 4.1: Ideal Day Vision
If you could design a day that honors your values, energy, and needs, what would it include?

Morning:
- Wake up time:
- First activity:
- Morning mood/energy:

Midday:
- Main focus/work:
- Social interaction level:

- Wind-down activity:

#### Exercise 4.2: Life Area Audit
Rate 1-10 how satisfied you are in each area:
- Health & Energy: ___
- Career/Purpose: ___
- Finances: ___
- Relationships: ___
- Personal Growth: ___
- Fun & Recreation: ___
- Environment (home/space): ___

For any rated 6 or below:
- Why is this area struggling?
- What would a "7" look like?
- What's ONE action toward that 7?

#### Exercise 4.3: Future Self Letter
Write a letter to yourself one year from now.

Include:
- What you hope to feel (not just achieve)
- What you want to have let go of

- What you're proud of yourself for

## General Workbook Design Principles

### Visual Design
- Large fonts (16pt minimum)
- Plenty of white space
- Color-coded sections
- Icons and emojis for visual breaks
- Fillable PDF fields

### ADHD-Friendly Features
- No overwhelming walls of text
- Clear progress indicators
- "Skip this if it doesn't resonate" permissions
- Optional vs required sections
- Time estimates for each exercise

### Accessibility
- Screen reader compatible PDFs
- High contrast options
- Printable versions
- Digital and analog options
- Mobile-friendly Notion versions

### Integration with Main System
- Each workbook references relevant Notion dashboard sections
- Completed exercises can be saved into Notion
- Prompts can trigger AI assistance
- Results inform dashboard customization
